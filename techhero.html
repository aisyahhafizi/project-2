Joy Buolamwini

Short Biodata

Joy Buolamwini, born on March 14, 1989 is a Canadian-American computer scientist, researcher and digital activist. She founded the Algorithmic Justice League (AJL) to combat bias in AI systems and advocate for ethical and inclusive technology. Her research revealed that facial recognition and other AI tools often perform poorly on darker-skinnes individuals and women, exposing risks of discrimination in areas like law enforcement and hiring. As a PhD graduate from MIT's Media Lab, she has published significant work and delivered talks globally to advocate about inclusivity to influence policymakers and tech companies Joy Buolamwini is recognized as a leading voice in ethical AI, promoting accountability and fairness in technology.

Manal al-Sharif

Short Biodata

Manal al-Sharif, born in 1979 in Mecca, Saudi Arabia, is a women’s rights activist and author. A computer security specialist who worked for Saudi Aramco, she became known worldwide after co-founding the #Women2Drive campaign in 2011, challenging Saudi Arabia’s ban on women driving. Though arrested for her activism, she inspired global attention and later authored Daring to Drive: A Saudi Woman’s Awakening. Recognized by TIME as one of the 100 Most Influential People, Manal al-Sharif continues to advocate for digital freedom, gender equality, and social justice.

Reshma Saujani

Short Biodata

Reshma Saujani, born on November 18, 1975, is an American lawyer, activist, and author. She is the founder of Girls Who Code, a nonprofit that works to close the gender gap in technology by empowering young women to learn coding skills. The daughter of Indian immigrants, she studied at Harvard and Yale before launching her career in public service. Reshma is also the founder of Moms First and the author of Brave, Not Perfect, encouraging girls and women to embrace risk, courage, and leadership. She is a leading advocate for women in STEM and economic empowerment.
